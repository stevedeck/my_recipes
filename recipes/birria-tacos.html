Birria Tacos!

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of beef chuck roast or goat meat
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 4 dried guajillo chili peppers
  • 4 dried ancho chili peppers
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 6 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 3 cups beef broth
  • Corn tortillas
  • Cilantro, chopped
  • White onion, chopped
  • Lime wedges
  • Queso fresco (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350Â°F.
  2. Remove the stems and seeds from the dried chili peppers, and then soak them in hot water for about 20 minutes, until they're soft.
  3. In a blender or food processor, puree the soaked chili peppers and diced tomatoes until they form a smooth sauce.
  4. In a large oven-safe pot or Dutch oven, combine the beef, chili-tomato sauce, smoked paprika, cumin, oregano, salt, black pepper, onion, garlic, and beef broth.
  5. Cover the pot with a lid and bake in the oven for about 3 hours, or until the meat is tender and falls apart easily.
  6. Remove the pot from the oven and shred the meat using two forks.
  7. To assemble the tacos, warm the tortillas in a skillet or on a griddle until they're soft and pliable.
  8. Spoon some of the birria meat onto each tortilla, and then top with chopped cilantro, onion, and crumbled queso fresco (if using).
  9. Serve the tacos with lime wedges on the side, and enjoy!
  10. To make consomme, simply strain the cooking liquid through a fine-mesh strainer and serve it in bowls alongside the tacos.
